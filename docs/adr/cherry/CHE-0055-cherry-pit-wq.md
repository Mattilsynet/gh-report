## Context

This ADR supersedes CHE-0052. Four problems surfaced between CHE-0052's acceptance (2026-05-09) and 2026-05-12 under `gh-report` use; all are rectified by this ADR's Decision and R1–R16 (oracle-ratified; provenance in git history): (1) `cherry-pit-runtime` misframes a worker-pool back-pressure stack as runtime-owning (CHE-0052:R5, CHE-0051:R8); (2) `pagination` (RFC 5988 `Link:` parsing) is a GitHub/HTTP adapter unrelated to the queue; (3) `RateLimitState::update_from_headers` hardcodes `x-ratelimit-*`, blocking second consumers; (4) `DomainKey`, `JobSource`, `JobOutcome<R>` belong in `cherry-pit-core` per CHE-0018:69-70 (oracle Q2 resolves CHE-0018:R1 "sync domain" ambiguity). The bundled fix also closes the CHE-0052:R4 / CHE-0052:R6 deferrals in v0.1 and lands as a single SemVer-major event; plan-mode G1–G6 ratified each bullet.

## Decision

`cherry-pit-wq` ships as the renamed successor to `cherry-pit-runtime`, with `WorkQueue<C>`, `JobSpec<C>`, `EnqueueResult`, `BatchEnqueueResult`, `BatchTracker`, `enqueue_batch`, `JobExecutor`, `WorkerPoolConfig`, `run_worker_pool`, `shutdown_worker_pool`, `BudgetGate`, `RateLimitState`, and the new `RateLimitObserver` trait as the v0.1 public surface, plus `pub use cherry_pit_core::{DomainKey, JobSource, JobOutcome};` re-exports for caller continuity. `DomainKey`, `JobSource`, and `JobOutcome<R>` are owned by `cherry-pit-core` from v0.1 of the renamed crate. The `pagination` module and the `HALT_THRESHOLD` / `WARN_THRESHOLD` constants leave the shared crate; `pagination` relocates to `gh-report::github::pagination` and the thresholds to `gh-report::github::rate_limit`. `RateLimitState::update_from_headers` is hard-cut in the rename window; gh-report supplies `GitHubHeaderObserver: RateLimitObserver` as the GitHub-specific adapter. `JobSpec<C>` carries `pub correlation: CorrelationContext` as a load-bearing field in v0.1, and `JobOutcome<R>` carries `correlation: CorrelationContext` on both `Success` and `Failure` variants — the CHE-0052 v0.2 deferral is closed by this ADR. Every paid-for break — rename, value-type relocation, R4/R6 reinstatement, pagination relocation, `update_from_headers` hard cut, threshold-constant move, `JobSource::InitialLoad` deletion — occurs in a single SemVer-major event per the target doc's bundle-discipline paragraph. The crate remains runtime-neutral (the consumer's binary owns `#[tokio::main]` per CHE-0051:R8 precedent) and in-process only.

R1 [5]: cherry-pit-wq's `[dependencies]` are `cherry-pit-core`, `tokio`, `tracing`, `http`, `scc`, `futures-util`, `tokio-util` ONLY (`tokio-util` supplies consumer-owned `CancellationToken` observation, not runtime construction); no other cherry-pit crate depends on it in v0.1; only consumer crates may. Peer-adapter wq deps require explicit DAG amendment.

R2 [5]: crate name is `cherry-pit-wq`. The rename is SemVer-major per CHE-0052:R11 + CHE-0030:R2; the name covers queue + pool + budget + rate-limit. Oracle Q1 permits v0.1 (CHE-0052 has zero inbound refs). The type-ownership decision driving the rename is binding in this ADR's Decision and R3 (DomainKey/JobSource/JobOutcome<R> owned by cherry-pit-core, re-exported from wq).

R3 [5]: `DomainKey`, `JobSource`, `JobOutcome<R>` relocate from `cherry-pit-runtime` to `cherry-pit-core` — additive on core (no methods, no async runtime dep, satisfying CHE-0018:R3), removed from wq with `pub use cherry_pit_core::{DomainKey, JobSource, JobOutcome};` re-exports preserving caller continuity per CHE-0030:R1 flat re-export discipline. Oracle Q2 resolves the CHE-0018:R1 "sync domain" ambiguity in favour of move-permitted, citing CHE-0018:69-70.

R4 [5]: `JobSpec<C>` carries `pub correlation: CorrelationContext` as a load-bearing v0.1 field; `JobSpec::new` and `enqueue_batch` accept it explicitly per CHE-0039:R2 (no `Default`); `JobExecutor::execute` keeps its `(&DomainKey, &Self::Context)` shape and reads `job.correlation` inside the body — option (b) of BC-7 alternatives per CHE-0052:R4's three reasons (minimal trait surface, batch-as-correlation-unit, parity with `EventEnvelope`). **Binding v0.1; closes CHE-0052 v0.2 deferral**; surprise bead `adr-fmt-tm6m`.

R5 [5]: cherry-pit-wq is runtime-neutral — no constructor calls `tokio::runtime::Runtime::new()` or `Builder::*`; `run_worker_pool` and `WorkQueue::dequeue` assume an active tokio runtime; consumer's binary owns `#[tokio::main]` and signal handling per CHE-0051:R8. No `start()`/`run()`/`wait_for_shutdown_signal` helper; `axum::serve` graceful shutdown stays in `gh-report`. CHE-0018:R3 holds (wq depends on core, not the reverse). G4 declined to extend the charter.

R6 [5]: `JobOutcome<R>` (now in `cherry-pit-core`) carries `correlation: CorrelationContext` on both `Success` and `Failure`, so dead-letter sinks, delivery tasks, and metrics emitters see the same chain as the executor. BC-7 holds across cancellation/error: a cancelled, panicking, or `Err`-returning job yields `Failure { correlation == JobSpec::correlation }`, satisfying CHE-0046 telemetry. **Binding v0.1; closes CHE-0052 v0.2 deferral**; surprise bead `adr-fmt-tm6m`.

R7 [5]: `pagination` relocates to `gh-report::github::pagination` (pagination relocation precedes the rename; both land in the single SemVer-major event per R8). **cherry-pit-wq contains NO `pagination` module — absence is load-bearing.** `HALT_THRESHOLD`/`WARN_THRESHOLD` move to `gh-report::github::rate_limit` per oracle Q4 (5000/hour defaults are GitHub-shaped, contradicting CHE-0052's domain-agnostic charter); wq retains `RateLimitState::with_thresholds(...)`. `JobSource::InitialLoad` is deleted per oracle Q7 (zero call sites; CHE-0021 symmetric discipline).

R8 [5]: every paid-for SemVer-major break lands in a SINGLE SemVer-major event — rename, value-type moves, R4/R6 reinstatement, pagination relocation, `update_from_headers` hard cut, threshold move, `JobSource::InitialLoad` deletion — under bundle discipline: fragmenting across patches multiplies cost without dividing work. This formalises CHE-0030:R2 and is what CHE-0022:R1's additive-only-minor floor implies for the bracketing minors.

R9 [4]: cherry-pit-wq gains `pub trait RateLimitObserver` with `observe(&self, headers: &http::HeaderMap, state: &RateLimitState)`, decoupling header parsing from the state machine; a second consumer with `RateLimit-Reset` (draft-ietf-httpapi-ratelimit-headers) or non-HTTP signals supplies its own impl without forking. gh-report provides `GitHubHeaderObserver: RateLimitObserver`. `RateLimitState::update_from_headers` is hard-cut (no deprecation) per oracle Q3: CHE-0052:R11's additive-only floor is overridden by R8's bundle-discipline major.

R10 [5]: v0.1 public surface per CHE-0030:R1 flat re-exports: from `cherry-pit-core` — `DomainKey`, `JobSource`, `JobOutcome`; from wq — `WorkQueue`, `JobSpec`, `EnqueueResult`, `BatchEnqueueResult`, `BatchTracker`, `enqueue_batch`, `JobExecutor`, `WorkerPoolConfig`, `run_worker_pool`, `shutdown_worker_pool`, `BudgetGate`, `RateLimitState`, `RateLimitObserver`. **Removed from CHE-0052:R3:** `next_url`, `next_url_same_origin`, `HALT_THRESHOLD`, `WARN_THRESHOLD` (now in gh-report per R7). Internal modules (`work_queue`, `worker_pool`, `budget`, `rate_limit` — `pagination` deleted) stay implementation detail per CHE-0030:R2.

R11 [4]: cherry-pit-wq carries `#![forbid(unsafe_code)]` at the crate root per CHE-0007:R1 and CHE-0007:R3, and contains no `unsafe` blocks, `unsafe impl`, or `unsafe fn` bodies per CHE-0007:R2 — CHE-0052:R2 carried forward unchanged; BC-14 is satisfied by construction

R12 [4]: graceful shutdown follows the donor `shutdown_worker_pool(handles, timeout)` contract — workers complete their current `JobExecutor::execute` future or are aborted at the timeout boundary — and reconciles with CHE-0046:R5: cancellation of a worker future does NOT imply rollback of any side effect the executor body may have committed before the cancellation point; recovery semantics are the consumer's responsibility (CHE-0052:R7 carried forward unchanged)

R13 [4]: cherry-pit-wq is in-process only — `WorkQueue` state is held in tokio mpsc and an `scc::HashSet`; process restart drops the queue. Durability is the consumer's job via an `EventStore`-backed outbox per CHE-0024 persist-then-publish ordering. Distributed queues, cross-process hand-off, and persistent job state remain deferred to v0.2 or a pardosa-tier transport ADR (CHE-0052:R8 unchanged).

R14 [4]: cherry-pit-wq emits observability via `tracing` only — no Prometheus exporter, no OpenTelemetry façade, no metrics-trait abstraction in v0.1; `WorkerPoolConfig` does not carry a metrics field; consumers needing structured metrics subscribe to the existing `tracing` events and bridge them with their own subscriber (CHE-0052:R9 carried forward unchanged)

R15 [4]: tests follow the CHE-0038 taxonomy — unit tests beside their modules, integration tests under `crates/cherry-pit-wq/tests/`, `tempfile` plus real tokio runtimes preferred over mocks per CHE-0038:R5 (CHE-0052:R10 unchanged). The BC-7 propagation property test asserting correlation parity between `JobSpec` and the resulting `JobOutcome` is now binding (per R4/R6) rather than aspirational.

R16 [4]: the public surface is additive-only across SemVer-minor bumps per CHE-0022:R1 — adding public types or re-exports is minor; renaming or removing any R10 item is major. `#[non_exhaustive]` markers preserved from the donor (`JobSpec`, `EnqueueResult`, `WorkerPoolConfig`, `BatchEnqueueResult`, `BatchTracker`, `RateLimitState`, `BudgetGate`) keep struct surfaces extensible; `JobSource`, `JobOutcome` (now in core) carry the same markers (CHE-0052:R11 retargeted in scope).

## Consequences

**Positive.** Name fits; core owns `DomainKey`/`JobSource`/`JobOutcome`. Pagination/threshold relocations honour the domain-agnostic charter. `RateLimitObserver` admits reuse without forking. R4/R6 close the CHE-0052 v0.2 deferral (G5). Breaks land in one SemVer-major (R8).

**Negative.** One-shot migration: dep rename, retargeted `pub use cherry_pit_wq::{…}` shims in gh-report, pagination/threshold path changes, `update_from_headers` callers → `GitHubHeaderObserver::observe(...)`. R4/R6 add `CorrelationContext` to `JobSpec::new`/`enqueue_batch` and `correlation` to `JobOutcome::{Success, Failure}` — surfacing correlation forgetting at compile time per CHE-0039. `JobSource::InitialLoad` deletion (R7) is source-incompatible; zero call sites.

**Open / deferred.** FLO-0001 (WSJF) deferred to v0.2 — name the trade here and accept the eventual SemVer-major; speculative `Option<CostOfDelay>` rejected per CHE-0022. R13 (durable queues), R14 (metrics façade) stay v0.2. Sagas deferred per CHE-0040. Donor `aggregate_store`/`event_bus` superseded by `cherry-pit-gateway::MsgpackFileStore` and `cherry-pit-app::InProcessEventBus` (CHE-0051:R2).

## Rejected Alternatives

**Inline-absorb the runtime primitives into gh-report (D1.a of the WU-6 plan-mode decision matrix).** Would have collapsed all five donor modules into `crates/gh-report/src/infra/` with no cherry-pit-named crate. Rejected at plan-mode ratification of CHE-0052 (D1.b chosen) and rejected again here: the worker-pool primitives are domain-agnostic by design (the donor lib.rs explicitly said "domain-agnostic building blocks for data collection pipelines"), and burying them inside an application crate forfeits any future cherry-pit consumer's ability to reuse them. The rename to `cherry-pit-wq` reinforces rather than weakens this — the new name describes the contents precisely, so the case for collapsing them into the application crate is weaker than it was at CHE-0052 acceptance.

**Single combined `cherry-pit-infra` crate covering both worker-pool primitives and storage primitives** (the parallel CHE-0052 A3 sub-mission). Rejected again: the two clusters have orthogonal dependency profiles — cherry-pit-wq requires tokio (CHE-0029:R5 adapter posture); cherry-pit-storage is pure I/O on `std::fs` and does not need tokio. Combining them would force every consumer of the storage primitives to take a tokio dependency, inflating the BC-13 transitive closure of any storage-primitive consumer for no benefit and violating the spirit of CHE-0029:R4's leaf-discipline reasoning (carried forward verbatim from CHE-0052's rejection of the same alternative).

**Keep donor `quics-aggregate` symbols in place under a re-export shim, with `cherry-pit-runtime` retained alongside `cherry-pit-wq`.** Would have left `cherry-pit-runtime` in the workspace as a deprecated alias of `cherry-pit-wq`. Rejected because (i) the cherry-pit-naming + DAG-hygiene commitment requires the symbols to live under a single cherry-pit-named crate, not two; (ii) the shim would create two public surfaces for the same types — a maintenance liability with no compensating benefit. Hard cut per oracle Q3.

**Add `CorrelationContext` as a parameter on `JobExecutor::execute` (option (a) of the BC-7 alternatives).** Would have changed the trait method signature to `execute(&self, &DomainKey, &Self::Context, CorrelationContext) -> Future<…>`. Rejected in favour of option (b) for the same three reasons CHE-0052's R4 records — minimal trait surface, batch-as-correlation-unit affordance, parity with `EventEnvelope` correlation on the storage side. Option (a) remains a reasonable design and could be added later as a parallel convenience without breaking option (b)'s consumers; option (b) is the load-bearing surface in v0.1 of cherry-pit-wq.

**cherry-pit-wq owns `#[tokio::main]` via a `RuntimeHarness::run_to_completion()` entrypoint** (G4 from plan-mode evaluation; also the rejected CHE-0051 Q7 alternate (a) and CHE-0052's rejected `cherry-pit-runtime` charter extension). Would have made the crate a turn-key worker-pool application surface. Rejected for the same reason a third time: the consumer's binary needs to compose the worker pool with HTTP (`cherry-pit-web::build_router`), with `cherry-pit-app::App::run`, and with signal handling that may need to coordinate across all three async surfaces. Runtime ownership in cherry-pit-wq would force the consumer to rebuild the runtime topology if any second async surface is added, exactly the cost CHE-0051:R8 already declined to pay. R5 of this ADR enshrines runtime-neutrality unchanged from CHE-0052.

**Park `Option<CostOfDelay>` field on `JobSpec` now (FLO-0001 prep; the speculative-field alternative to the deferral named in Open/Deferred).** Would have added a speculative additive field on `JobSpec` to reduce the eventual FLO-0001 conformance to a SemVer-minor event. Rejected per CHE-0022:R1's caution against speculative public-API surface — the cost-of-delay shape is itself a v0.2 design problem (FLO-0001 alone does not pin the field type), and committing to `Option<CostOfDelay>` now would either constrain the FLO-0001 design or force a SemVer-major break anyway when the field type proved wrong. Option A (name the trade in this ADR's Open/Deferred section, accept the eventual SemVer-major) is the chosen path per oracle's strategic-deferral recommendation.
